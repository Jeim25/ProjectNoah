About Project Noah

Our Purpose

Project Noah is a sophisticated IoT Sewer Monitoring System designed to mitigate the risks of urban flooding and drainage failures. By deploying a network of smart sensors across critical infrastructure—starting with the PUP Sta. Mesa campus—we provide real-time visibility into the pulse of the city's underground waterways.

Real-time Monitoring

Continuous tracking of water levels and flow rates with sub-second precision updates.

Early Warning

Automated alerts triggered when thresholds are breached, enabling proactive disaster response.

Capabilities

  • Dynamic Node Management: Easily scale the monitoring network by adding or removing nodes via an interactive map interface.
  • Environmental Simulation: Test system resilience with advanced typhoon and flood scenarios to prepare for extreme weather.
  • Intelligent Analytics: Localized data storage ensures historical patterns and notifications are preserved across sessions.

Node Status Definitions

Normal

Level < Threshold AND Flow > Threshold. Optimal drainage conditions.

Warning

Level > Threshold AND Flow > Threshold. High capacity usage detected.

Blockage

Level > Threshold AND Flow < Threshold. Critical failure likely due to debris or obstruction.
